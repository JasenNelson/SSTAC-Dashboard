# Matrix Sediment Standards Derivation Options Paper: Master Outline

## 1.0 Executive Summary
- High-level overview of the BC Ministry of Environment's mandate to modernize sediment quality guidelines.
- Summary of the four primary matrix derivation pathways (Ecological/Human Health, Direct Contact/Food Web).
- Key recommendations and selected derivation approach presented for the province.

## 2.0 Context & Problem Statement
- **Limitations of Empirical Approaches:** Discussion of the shortcomings of the status quo (e.g., correlative methods failing to account for bioavailability).
- **The Matrix Framework Need:** Rationale for transitioning to a multi-pathway, mechanistic matrix framework.
- **Scope & Objectives:** Defining the endpoints, receptors, and regulatory integration goals for Phase 2.

## 3.0 Jurisdictional Scan & Scientific Frameworks
- **Ecological - Direct Contact:** Analysis of Equilibrium Partitioning (EqP) and Acid Volatile Sulfide (AVS) normalization (US EPA, CCME).
- **Ecological - Food Web:** Review of Biota-Sediment Accumulation Factors (BSAF) and trophic transfer modeling (California SWRCB).
- **Human Health Pathways:** Derivations protecting human populations from dermal contact and incidental ingestion.

## 4.0 Evaluation Criteria
- **Scientific Defensibility:** Robustness of the mathematical models and peer-reviewed acceptance.
- **Feasibility & Implementation:** Practicality of field data collection (e.g., TOC, AVS) and laboratory analytical limits.
- **Indigenous Integration:** Alignment with Indigenous rights, values, and localized traditional consumption practices.

## 5.0 Proposed Matrix Implementation Options
- **Option 1: Status Quo:** Maintaining empirical, co-occurrence-based guidelines (baseline reference).
- **Option 2: Full Mechanistic:** Adopting strict EqP and BSAF modeling across all sediment types and regions.
- **Option 3: Tiered Hybrid Approach:** Utilizing empirical screening values combined with site-specific mechanistic derivations for high-risk or complex sites.

## 6.0 Indigenous Knowledge Integration
- **Two-Eyed Seeing Approach:** Weaving traditional knowledge with western mechanistic science.
- **Consumption Modifiers:** Integrating high-volume traditional aquatic food consumption rates into standard Human Health equations.
- **Collaborative Governance:** Ensuring Indigenous participation in site-specific derivation modifications.

## 7.0 Recommendations & Next Steps
- **TWG Review Process:** Aggregation of Technical Working Group feedback and polling data from the dashboard.
- **Final Selection:** Official recommendation of the preferred implementation option to the province.
- **Phase 3 Roadmap:** Timeline for regulatory drafting, public consultation, and ultimate policy implementation.
